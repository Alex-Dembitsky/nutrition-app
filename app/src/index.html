Order Matters

Ingredients are listed in descending order by weight, so the first ingredient is present in the largest amount. Pay attention to what’s at the top of the list.

  • potatoes
  • salt
  • oil
  • whey
  • maltodextrin
  • pepper
  • paprika

What types of ingredients are important to look out for?

All About Grains

The FDA recommends we get at least half of the grains we eat daily from whole grains. But how do you know if you’re eating a whole grain?

Whole Grains

Contain the entire grain kernel (and all of those good nutrients and fiber).

Examples of Whole grains

  • Whole Wheat
  • Wild Rice
  • Popcorn
  • Oats/Oatmeal
  • Millet
  • Bulgur

And if you want to make sure a product has only whole grains — look for 100% Whole Wheat/Grain on the package!

Refined Grains

Processed and stripped of essential B vitamins and fiber.

Examples of food made with refined grain

  • White Rice
  • Need examples of refined grains
  • White flour
  • White pasta

When a grain is “enriched”, B vitamins are added back in but the fiber is not.

Genetically Modified Ingredients

Genetically modified organisms (GMOs) in which the genetic material (DNA) is altered in a way that’s meant to translate into a product with a lower price, greater benefit (like durability or nutritional value) or both.

The 5 Crops

There’s a big debate (that we’re not going to get into!) around the impact of GMOs on both our health and the environment. The interesting thing to know is that GMOs are concentrated in 5 core crops.

  • Corn

  • Cotton

  • Canola

  • Soy

  • Sugar Beet
